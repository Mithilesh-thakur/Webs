Best Historical Places
in Bharat



Bharat is a country steeped in history and culture,with a
berich tapestry of historical places that spanbr
thousands of years.



Taj Mahal is essentially a tomb built in the memory of Shah Jahan's wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Taj Mahal is also famous as the symbol of love.
The Red Fort is located in Delhi, opposite the Chandni Chowk market. It was built by the famous Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1648. It is one of the historical structures of Delhi, which is mainly known for its architecture. Due to the use of red sandstone and marble, it is known as Red Fort.
Kedarnath is considered a very special and holy place for Hindus, and is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India. Located in Rudraprayag district in Uttarakhand, Kedarnath is a special place that attracts millions of visitors each year. Spirituality of Kedarnath, when combined with stunning natural beauty, makes it almost impossible to ignore.

Khajuraho Temples, Madhya Pradesh: The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is famous for its intricate and explicit erotic sculptures. These temples were built by the Chandela dynasty in the 10th century and are dedicated to Hindu and Jain deities.
Hampi, Karnataka: Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the site of the ancient Vijayanagara Empire. It boasts incredible ruins of temples, markets, and palaces spread across a surreal boulder-strewn landscape.
Jaipur City Palace, Rajasthan: Located in the Pink City of Jaipur, the City Palace is a magnificent complex of palaces, courtyards, and museums. It showcases the grandeur of Rajput architecture and offers insights into the royal history of Rajasthan.
Qutub Minar, Delhi: Standing as a testament to the Delhi Sultanate's architectural prowess, the Qutub Minar is a towering minaret built in the 12th century. It's surrounded by other historical structures and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Amer Fort, Rajasthan: Located on a hilltop in Jaipur, Amer Fort is a majestic fort-palace known for its stunning architecture, including the Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors) and the intricate Ganesh Pol.
Mysore Palace, Karnataka: The Mysore Palace is a splendid example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. It's the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and features opulent interiors and beautifully landscaped gardens.
